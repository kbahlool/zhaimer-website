About Zhaimer

Zhaimer is an original strategic memory card game created by Khalid Bahlool. It combines hidden information, memory, timing and calculated risk. The rules are simple to learn, but every round demands careful decisions.

The objective

Finish the round with the lowest total value among your hidden cards. During the game, remember your cards, replace high-value cards, use special powers wisely and decide when it is safe to declare that you are finished.

Starting a round

Each player receives four face-down cards. At the beginning, players briefly view two of their cards and must then rely on memory. On each turn, a player draws a card and decides how to use it according to the game options shown on screen.

Card values

  • Number cards normally count as their printed number.
  • Picture cards normally count as 10 points.
  • The 10 of Hearts and 10 of Diamonds count as 0 points.
  • The lower your final total, the better.

Special cards

  • King: gives a draw-two ability.
  • Jack: allows cards to be swapped.
  • Queen: allows a hidden card to be revealed once.

Matching and discarding

Matching cards can be discarded when the game allows it. A failed match is penalized, so memory and attention matter. The red zero-value tens form a special matching pair under Zhaimer’s rules.

Ending the round

When a player believes they have the lowest total, they can declare the round finished. The remaining players receive their final turn, then all cards are revealed and scores are calculated.

Ways to play

Practise against computer-controlled opponents or use the available online-room mode to play with friends. Local leaderboard information is stored in the browser and does not require an account.

Future development

Zhaimer Labs plans to keep improving the game with clearer tutorials, polished visuals, stronger accessibility, additional statistics and new ways to compete.
